Three core problems addressed
Scale-up gapLab-validated processes fail at pilot scale due to biological variability and insufficient process intelligence. Oxygen transfer limitation is an active bottleneck at the current 300L pilot.
Digital tools gapEnterprise-grade Digital Twin platforms (Siemens, AVEVA, AspenTech) are too expensive and complex for European bio-based SMEs working with variable feedstocks.
Circular feedstock gapVariable substrates (lignocellulosic hydrolysates, food surplus, acetogenates) are operationally unstable. No validated Digital Twin addresses this configuration.
Three strategic differentiators
Hohenheim biochemical modelExisting validated mechanistic model of surfactin biosynthesis — serves as the Digital Twin core. No competitor has this asset.
BACIPRO exploitation pathwayParallel EU project (July 2025) developing Bacillus-based agricultural products — provides direct TRL 7 route and end-user base for surfactin formulations.
SME-deployable architectureDigital Twin designed for deployment without external consultants: open standards, FAIR data, modular toolkit. Target payback: 12–24 months.

EFQM w SURFactory — Model EFQM 2020 jest frameworkiem zarządzania ciągłym doskonaleniem wdrożonym przez Fundację Rozwoju UE Wrocław, jedyną organizację w Polsce z trzema statusami EFQM jednocześnie. W projekcie pełni rolę warstwy governance — spaja pętlę DBTL, Digital Twin i replikowalność dla MŚP.
Co to jest EFQM Model 2020

Model EFQM (European Foundation for Quality Management) to niepreskryptywny standard doskonałości organizacyjnej, który integruje przywództwo, strategię, ludzi, partnerstwa, procesy, innowacje i wyniki. Ocena opiera się na logice RADAR (Results, Approaches, Deploy, Assess and Refine).

Model 2020 organizuje doskonałość w trzech wymiarach i siedmiu kryteriach:

Direction — Kierunek (Dlaczego?)Kryterium 1: Purpose, Vision & Strategy — jasność celu, strategia, dopasowanie do potrzeb interesariuszy.
Kryterium 2: Organisational Culture & Leadership — wartości, przywództwo, governance i etyka.
Execution — Wykonanie (Jak?)Kryterium 3: Engaging Stakeholders — tworzenie wartości z klientami, ludźmi, partnerami, społeczeństwem.
Kryterium 4: Creating Sustainable Value — procesy, produkty, usługi.
Kryterium 5: Driving Performance & Transformation — innowacje, technologie, zarządzanie wynikami.
Results — Wyniki (Co?)Kryterium 6: Stakeholder Perceptions — jak interesariusze postrzegają organizację.
Kryterium 7: Strategic & Operational Performance — osiągnięte wyniki strategiczne i operacyjne.

Tekst do wniosku — sekcja Methodology (gotowy fragment)

EFQM/RADAR governance methodology

SURFactory applies the EFQM 2020 Model as its continuous improvement governance framework, implemented by the Wroclaw University of Economics Foundation — the only organisation in Poland simultaneously holding EFQM Certified Recognition Organisation, Certified Training Organisation and Advisor Organisation status. This unique combination of competences ensures that EFQM is not applied as a formal compliance exercise, but as an operational excellence tool embedded in daily project decisions.

The EFQM Direction–Execution–Results logic is mapped directly onto the project architecture: Direction corresponds to the strategic positioning of the Digital Twin as an SME-accessible industrial competitiveness tool and the definition of the DBTL learning loop as the project's core operating logic; Execution corresponds to pilot campaigns, model recalibration cycles, stakeholder co-creation sprints and Open Innovation Access Programme onboarding; Results are tracked through the R1–R6 KPI framework with RADAR assessments conducted at each project milestone.

The RADAR logic (Results → Approaches → Deploy → Assess → Refine) ensures that deviations from KPI targets trigger structured corrective actions rather than ad hoc responses, and that all validated process and Digital Twin outputs are documented in replicable, evidence-based formats suitable for SME deployment and policy evidence packages. Student-led living labs at the Wroclaw University of Economics Foundation implement challenge-based co-creation activities, integrating societal and environmental acceptance considerations into daily project decisions rather than treating them as end-of-project communication tasks.

This approach has been successfully applied in the BACIPRO project within the same consortium and received positive recognition from reviewers — providing a validated precedent for its application in SURFactory at a larger scale and with broader European reach.
